Red Oak Benches

Aug 25, 2008

My neighbor back in New York needed some help clearing some land for a new shed. My dad and I headed over with a chainsaw and truck to clear some of the larger trees. After the work was done, I took a log from one large red oak down to a sawmill to have some boards cut. The result was a stack of 1" live edge boards with a vibrant grain.

After a few months of drying, the boards were ready to go. I chose to keep the theme of the live edges consistent throughout the entire bench, and used some dried limbs to make the legs. Both benches were made use of press fit joints throughout, allowing me to assemble them without using a single nail or screw.
