Robinson Greig

Who have you almost become?

May 9, 2015

Almost.

Almost is a word that separates the defeated from the triumphant. It is a word that highlights both our greatest successes and our biggest regrets.

I almost dropped out of college.

I almost didn’t pursue my passion.

I almost gave up on that idea.

It expresses how we perform against conflict and endure through adversity.

I almost moved out.

I almost killed myself.

I almost burned that bridge.

It reveals so much about who we are as human beings.

I almost bought that homeless man breakfast.

I almost didn’t speak up when he hit her.

I almost let it happen.

It demonstrates our addiction or allergy to risk, uncertainty, or adventure.

I almost booked a one-way ticket.

I almost went skydiving.

I almost learned something new about the world.

It allows us to exaggerate the mundane and believe we are more interesting than we are.

I almost went for a run today.

I almost didn’t write this essay.

I almost broke my routine.

Some of us are satisfied by living vicariously. Indiana almost lost his hat, Maverick almost didn’t fly again, and Facebook offers an endless stream of someone else’s experiences.

Others create their own suspense. They pursue a challenge and stare fate in the face. They would rather be eaten than never run the risk of being eaten. They live for the experience; for the people they meet, the places they see, the impact they make, and the emotions they feel. They live and die on the edge of almost.

If you want to learn about yourself, pay attention to the things you have almost done or almost haven’t done. Which of those do you regret and which of those do you cherish? What can you learn about those experiences? What would you do differently next time?

What can you do right now to change?
